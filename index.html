Private violin, viola, cello, and double bass teacher; small group general music instruction.

About Me

I have been a musician for over 15 years, learning first the piano, then moving to my instrument, the viola. My desire to start a private music studio stems from my own love of music at an early age. I have always seen great value in private and small group lessons and I would like to be able to serve students and families in the Northwest Indianapolis area, and beyond.

I graduated from the Jacobs School of Music at Indiana University in 2014 with a Bachelor of Music Education, where I continued to refine my own musical skills while also learning how to teach music to all ages. I have taught as a general music teacher for the last two and a half years in both the Arizona and Indianapolis Public School system.

I specialize in teaching elementary and middle school string students (violin, viola, cell, and double bass), and preschool to second grade general music classes.

Expectations

Expecations for Me:

Expecations for Students and Parents:

My Best Instruction

I will give my best instruction to the student by maintaining uninterrupted lesson time, being prepared for each lesson, and differentiating my instruction to accommodate each student.

Preparation

Students will be ready to learn and try new things at each lesson and will treat each lesson time as instructional time.

Practice Guides

I will create a practice guide for both student and parent to use throughout the week.

Practice

Students will practice at least 20 minutes, four times per week, minimum, and will keep a log of what has been practiced; if a student cannot write, we will create symbols for the student to notate.

Professionalism

I will be professional, respectful, and caring towards each student and parent.

Attendance

Parents will always be in attendance and in the room during the lesson. Parents will take notes during the lesson to help throughout the week.
